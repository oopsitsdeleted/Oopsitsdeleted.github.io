Custom mechanical keyboards are, well, keyboards. Except, these ones are more premium than your generic keyboard that probably came with your computer. 

Mechanical means that under each letter is a mechanical part. This mechanical part is called a "switch". A switch registers the keypress. 

If you can take of the keycaps, your keyboard could just show a rubber dome. These rubber domes are cheap and easy to make. However, these keyboards with rubber domes are generally not as good as mechanical as it feels extremely mushy and cheap.

If you've seen those gaming keyboards with mechanical switches, you might think why custom?

Custom keyboards are recommended as you can choose literally everything. From materials, colours, feel and much more! However, with Razers for example, you can only choose the feel or the colour. That's it.

Part 1: What components do you need?

Part 1 will explain what parts you will need to build your very own custom mechanical keyboard.

Image Description

Switches.

As stated above, switches are the component that gives a keyboard it's sound and feel. 

These switches can cost from a few cents for 1 switch or a dollar for 1 switch.

There are a lot of brands that make switches. However, the most popular brands that enthusiasts use include Gateron, Durock, Drop and ZealPC.

We'll be focusing on Cherry or Kailh style switches as they're the most popular and the most used.

Different types of switches

Cherry MX blues. (Clicky)

These switches have a really loud audible "click" once you press down on it. These are most popular as a lot of gaming keyboards come with these switches.

Holy Pandas. (Tactile)

You might not have heard of these as they're for enthusiasts, Drop Holy Pandas give you a bump once you press down on it. 

Novelkeys Creams. (Linear)

Novelkeys (NK) creams are the most popular enthusiast switches as Tfue's custom keyboard came with it.
Linear switches are like tactile switches but without the bump.

What are the best switches?

Well, there are a lot of different preferences so it's up to you. If you really don't know what switches to get, I recommend you just watch videos on switches or keyboards.

But, these are the most popular types of switches if you are too lazy.

Most popular types of switches

Kailh Box Jades. (Clicky)

These are popular as they're way more clicky than Cherry MX blues and it gives a much more sophisticated sound signature.

Boba U4Ts. (Tactile)

These are popular as the sounds it gives are so so SO good. It also gives a pretty noticeable tactile bump unlike some other swtiches.

Gateron Ink Black. (Linear)

These are popular as it feels pretty smooth and it's easy to get. The sound signature also is way better than some other switches.

Then can you make your switches sound and feel better?

Well, yes! There are products that make your switches feel smoother and it can make the switches sound better.

Lubricant. (Lube)

I know, lube, yeah, you can stop laughing now. Lube can help your switches sound better and makes it feel more smooth. 

The opposite for smooth is "scratchy". Most people use the term scratchy when a switch doesn't sound smooth.

Image Description

Switch Films.

If you still want to improve your switch's sound and feel, you can spend a bit more for switch films. These are little pieces of rubber/plastic that you put in between the switch to make the plus thingy (stem) less wobbly and it makes the switch sound more deep.

Image Description

Keycaps.

You touch this part when you use the keyboard the most. So it's pretty important. There are a lot of different brands, materials, prices and colours that you can get.

Keycaps can cost quite a bit but if you look at the right place, it can cost as cheap as 20USD.

There aren't really popular brands for cheaper keycaps. But for more premium keycap brands there are GMK and ePBT. There are more premium keycap brands, but these brands are the most popular. Their keycaps can cost quite a bit but it is higher quality.

Different types of keycaps

ABS.

ABS is the most popular as it is the cheapest. The keyboard you have now may have ABS.

PBT.

PBT is the higher quality plastic as it doesn't become shiny as easily and it has a higher melting point than ABS.

Printing styles

There are a lot more printing styles than this, but these are the most popular printing styles that you would usually see.
Laser Etching.

Even those these look the coolest when making, they're the worst of the bunch. These will fade off after a while and it's found in cheap gaming keyboards or some office keyboards. DO NOT GET THESE!

Double-Shot.

Double-Shot printing uses 2 types of plastics. It is probably the best keycap printing style as the letters don't fade. 2 types of plastics are molded together to form the letters on keycaps.

Dye-Sublimation.

Dye-Sublimation (or Dye-sub) is similar to Double-Shot as it uses similar printing methods. The only difference is that this is only used on PBT keycaps and it uses dye or clay instead of plastic to form the letters.

Image Description

Case.

The case is what puts everything together. Every single component is put into here. The case is also responsible for the sound and the feel of switches.

Different types of cases

Plastic.

Plastic is used for a lot of keyboards as, you know, it's cheap.
But this doesn't mean it's bad, it can sound good but it doesn't feel as premium.

Metal (Aluminum, brass, steel etc).

A ton of custom keyboards use these as it feels the most premium and it is more heavy. It's also more durable.

Wood.

Wood is less common but probably only custom keyboards use this as most people won't like wood on their keyboards. 
Personally, I really recommend wood as it gives an interesting sound signature and it honestly feels really good.

Image Description

Printed Circuit Board.

Also known as PCB, this component is the most important as it literally sends info to your computer and gives your keyboard RGB. 

There aren't popular brands as it usually just comes with your keyboard.

Different types of PCBs

Soldered.

These are most popular, as, you guessed it, it's the cheapest. Most enthusiasts prefer this as it gives you experience. However, for beginners, most people recommend hotswap PCBs.

This means that once you put your switches in, you have to solder the switches onto the PCB.

Hotswap.

Hotswap PCBs means that you can just put your switches in and you're good to go. You don't have to solder.

Image Description

Plate.

Plates sit above the PCB and it holds the switches. The feel of your keyboard can also be affected by your plate.

There aren't popular brands, as this usually just comes with your keyboard.

Different types of plates

Polycarbonate.

Also known as plastic or PC. I don't have to explain why this is popular in normal keyboards lmao.

Custom keyboard builders also use this as it gives a softer typing feel and it gives a deeper sounding keyboard.

Aluminum.

Aluminum is a pretty good in-between for PC and Brass. 
While aluminum gives a more solid typing feel, it still gives a similar sound signature of a PC plate. 

Brass

Brass gives a really premium look. However, it gives a pretty solid feel and the sound signature is pretty high pitched which most people don't really like. These also cost more than all the other plates.

That's all the components you need.

Even though there are more if you're a hardcore mechanical keyboard enthusiast, this guide is for beginners.
